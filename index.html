I am a Ph.D candidate (expected 2011 graduation) in the Computer Science Department at Carnegie Mellon University, advised by Professor David Andersen. My interests span topics in networked computer systems, including low-power cluster systems, datacenter networking, and Internet measurement.

In this winter (man, this is very tough), I'm going to take my talents to South Bay and join Google.

Past Projects

As an undergraduate at Berkeley, I worked on two projects in Ken Goldberg's Berkeley Automation Lab. Specifically, I was the network engineer on the Ballet Mori project and also worked on physical deformation simulations for needle surgery planning. I also worked on DDoS prevention in next generation Internet architectures and implemented ConeNAT functionality within the OCALA framework.
